Wed Apr 1
Kyoto: Fushimi + Higashiyama Kyoto's most visual day
🌅 Start this day at 6:30–7 AM. Fushimi Inari is completely different (and magical) before tour groups arrive. The afternoon is then free for slower things.
Morning · Fushimi Inari
Must Do
  • Fushimi Inari Taisha — walk the 1,000 vermillion torii gates visual temple go by 7am — you don't need to hike all 4 hrs, the first 45 min is the iconic part
  • Vermillion Café nearby for coffee after the walk
Afternoon · Higashiyama & Gion
Must Do
  • Higashimuki / Ninenzaka cobblestone shopping street — traditional crafts, ceramics, snacks
  • Wife and Husband Café — famous local coffee shop, intimate and beautiful afternoon tea
Good To Do
  • Nanzenji Tenjuan — moss garden, stone lanterns, and a mirror-still koi pond tucked inside Nanzenji temple complex visual temple — this is landscape as art; serene, uncrowded, and 10 min walk from Kiyomizudera
  • Kiyomizudera temple — wooden stage jutting from the hillside, stunning architecture — combine with Tenjuan since they're walkable
  • Gion evening walk along Hanamikoji Street
If Time
  • Nishiki Market food stalls for dinner snacks
  • Hikiniku to Come — burger patty served over rice, Kyoto outpost of a Tokyo cult classic — great casual lunch option
  • Men ya Inoichi — refined, deeply flavoured ramen — one of Kyoto's most respected bowls; worth the visit if you're in the area
  • Kyoto Engine Ramen or Umishen Karaage for a casual dinner
Thu Apr 2
Kyoto: Arashiyama Day scenic railway + bamboo + river
All Day · Arashiyama
Must Do
  • Sagano Scenic Railway (Torokko Train) book ahead — 25 min ride through mountain gorge, especially gorgeous during sakura season
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — go immediately after the train before the crowds peak
  • Boat ride back down the Hozu River — leisurely return, stunning scenery
  • Riverside walk and lunch along Togetsukyo Bridge
Good To Do
  • Tenryu-ji temple garden visual temple — the garden is the attraction here, not the building; a UNESCO landscape of borrowed scenery
  • Thisis Shizen — flower ice cream in the area — if you see it, go
If Time
  • Donut stand in the Arashiyama region
  • Kimono rental for the afternoon walk

Sat Apr 4
Nara Day Trip ~45 min by train from Kyoto
🦌 Nara is compact and deeply special. The giant Buddha at Todai-ji is genuinely one of the most impressive sculptures you'll ever stand in front of — it's not religious, it's overwhelmingly human. Give yourself a full day.
Morning · Nara Park + Todai-ji
Must Do
  • Todai-ji Temple — the Great Buddha Hall houses Japan's largest bronze Buddha (15m tall) visual temple — genuinely awe-inspiring as architecture + sculpture regardless of faith
  • Nara Park deer — they bow for crackers, it's surreal and delightful
Good To Do
  • Kasuga Taisha — lantern-lined stone paths through ancient cedar forest — go downhill from here for fewer crowds, per your notes
  • Higashimuki Shopping Street — local souvenirs, Nara crafts
If Time
  • Dorgawa trolley route instead of main park area — from your notes, fewer tourists
  • Mt. Wakakusa for panoramic views of the city
Evening · Return to Kyoto
Must Do
  • Return to Kyoto for final dinner — Pontocho or anywhere you missed
  • Pack and prep for tomorrow's Shinkansen to Tokyo 🎂
